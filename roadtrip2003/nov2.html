Nov. 2nd, 3rd & 4th, 2003 -- Missoula > Salt Lake City

The snow continued overnight, but worrying over weather.com--our new best friend--paid off. Getting out of Missoula was a little slow, as anticipated, but once we got into the valley and crossed into Idaho the skies cleared and all was smooth sailing. It was hard to leave after just a tiny taste of Pete, Tina, Molly and Hannah, but we have a loose schedule to keep if we want to make NY by Thanksgiving. And, with the snow predicted, our new best friend said if we stayed an extra night we'd likely wind up staying an extra week. So, push off we did. Montana continued to delight all the way to the border, with tasteful roadside ads for Rocky Mountain Oysters. Our favorite billboard: "Rock Creek Lodge Testicle Festival. Have a Ball!"

The radio is a constant companion, though its offerings are limited to the guy sitting in for Rush Limbaugh and a cadre of conservative scumpatriots. Fortunately, we've dipped into the audio book of Al Franken's Lies and the Lying Liars who Tell Them, allowing for a fair and balanced automotive audio experience. Late in the afternoon, we delighted in the therapeutic offerings of Dr. Laura, who, surprisingly, seems not to fear diminishing her listening audience by providing callers with advice that might provoke a permanent exit of the Judy Garland variety.
 
It was one of the longer drives on this trip, but we'd left early enough to arrive at Jon and Alyssa's by dinner time. With three kids six and under, we thought it might help if we showed up with pizza in hand. This was our chance to test the capabilities of the Lexus Link system. Why fumble with the cell phone in the dark when someone in a clean, well-lighted call center could place the order? We needed a pizza place near the university. The one we remembered from our years living here was probably gone, gone, gone. Eric expressed his fear at using Lexus Link with his customary clarity.

"I'm afraid," he said.

But he summoned his courage and pushed the "Service" button.

A fellow chimed in from the car's speakers, "How may I help you, Mr. and Mrs. Benson?"

Positively creepy.

"We're looking for a pizza place near the University," I said.

"May I put you on hold while I look?" he asked.

Syrupy easy-listening filled the car. And even though I hadn't told him where we were, the Lexus Link guy knew, a fact even creepier than the music. He returned with a helpful list of chains. I asked if he could check on the pizza place we remembered, just to be sure it wasn't still there.

  Ever chipper, he asked, "May I put you on hold again?"

"Could you do it without the music?" I queried.

"Sorry, I can't," he said.

He returned with the expected news of Gepetto's demise, so we chose the only independent on the list.

"Then Litza's is fine. Could you please order two large cheese, with olives?"

"Sure," he said. "I'll need to put you on hold."

Moments later he came back with the news that the pizza would be waiting for us in 15 minutes. Amazing.

We navigated familiar streets with that haunting sense of what had been there. Some landmarks remain, but others have conspired to annoy by disappearing. And our worry about finding pizza was one of the stupidest ever hatched. In a four block stretch near Trolley Square we passed at least eight pizza joints. Unfortunately, none of them were Litza's, which we clearly remembered and had not moved. But so much had changed around it that it took a little doing to find it. Finally, as the Lexus Link Lady coos in her dulcet way, "we arrived at our destination." With pizza steaming up the car windows and soon-to-be-former Gov. Mike Leavitt giving his goodbye speech on the radio, we pulled up to Jon and Alyssa's. Jon was waving through the huge picture window in their living room. Aric, who is in a clothes-are-evil stage, stood on a chest under the window, peering out at us. (This window is so large that when I lived in the house I used to feel like I was on display in a diorama, placed there for the amusement of passersby on McClelland St. No doubt, Aric is far more entertaining to pedestrian traffic than we ever were.)

Jon and Alyssa's house crackles with kid energy. If there were a way to harness Aric's good spirit, you could light a small town. He'll turn four this weekend and we're told Batman will be an honored guest at the party. At 14 months, Sam is a meditative baby. He has much to observe and is clearly entertained by his sibs. Ainsley is happy, confident and as benevolent as an elder sister can be without losing her alpha status. Rufus, Murray and Choto (cousin dogs--in fact, Choto, a Corgi, is a true cousin) checked each other out. All respected Choto as queen bee. I was honored that Aric asked that I pick the olives off his slice of pizza. And the Chinese Character lesson on the living room floor had Uncle Eric remembering that at Ainsley's age, he had learned the character for man. After a little visit we rolled home to our hotel. Rufus tends to work a hotel lobby like he's running for office, but tonight, he allowed for how tired we were and made a beeline for the elevator.

The morning brought snow, but nothing much stuck. As the flakes fell we were consumed by getting this site happening. Jon joined us for lunch at Bambara early in the afternoon. (If ever you pass through, order their homemade blue cheese potato chips. I ate two and was glad I didn't live here anymore, as easy access to these would be a dangerous thing.) We strolled over to Jon's office at Avalanche Software and met some of the folks responsible for getting the company going. They've recently launched the game, Tak, to pretty glowing reviews. (Jon is a sound designer for the game and we're pretty glowing about him.)

Back at the ranch, Ainsley was finished with her Spanish class and ready to hang out. When we got to their house she was making a snowman in the back yard. Aric was patrolling the living room, and Gack was on the agenda. It's oozy, runny, gummy fun. Here's the recipe, in case you want to make some of your own:

Mix:

1 3/4 cups white glue
2 cups water
Food color

Separately mix:

2 T Borax
2/3 cup hot water

Add Borax mix to glue mix and work in with hands.

DON'T get this stuff on fabric. You'll be sorry.

Too much fun equals hunger. And when you're really hungry, what's more satisfying than Mexican food served up with indoor cliff diving? Nothing we could think of. And so, we headed to the The Mayan which is as kitsch as kitsch can be. Tucked away in a mall in suburban Salt Lake, even its Mayan temple-like outside in no way prepares you for what's inside. If you've ever been to Disneyland, it's a little like Pirates of the Carribean meets The Tiki Room meets an old Tarzan and Jane movie set. Or Chuck-E-Cheese meets Waterworld. A hostess walked us upstairs and through a maze of stone walls. We were seated at a table on one of many platforms jutting out by a three-story rock cliff, complete with waterfalls and a deep pool below. Very Swiss Family Robinson. As we perused the menu, high on a nearby rock, an animatronic toucan and parrot sang "Hot, Hot, Hot." The room darkened, thunder rumbled and faux lightening flashed. The waterfalls surged. And then, a diver appeared in the briefest of bikinis, looking for all the world like a romance novel cover boy. He was followed by a pal. And then the two dove with no small amount of skill and grace. It was impressive. But, just like Headline News, it repeated every 1/2 hour, with the business of being in a restaurant going on as if nothing else is happening. After the first couple of shows, it becomes routine, and you find yourself chatting away. Though you'll occasionally catch a falling body out of the corner of your eye and no matter how you slice it, that's disquieting. Between diving shows, a balloon animal clown stopped by the table and fashioned a credible unicorn for Ainsley and a sword for Aric, much to their delight. And every few minutes, another table would erupt in the Birthday Song. But the best and most confusing cross-cultural part of celebrating a birthday at The Mayan? You haven't lived until you've heard "La Cucaracha" played on a bagpipe.

Minutes spent listening to Muzak on hold with Lexus Link: 7

Favorite sighting: Naked Aric in the big picture window
