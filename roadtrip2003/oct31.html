Oct. 31, 2003 -- Seattle > Spokane

Months of planning and anticipation boiled down into an obligatory round of luggage Tetris, and off we went. Pulling out of our driveway felt both mundane and momentous. Goodbye Seattle. Hello ?

Stats:

Hours driving: 4
Best sighting: Our neighbor's daughter, dressed as a princess riding an unicorn, waving goodbye from the hill behind our house.
Media treats: NPR story on Candy: The Sweet History by Beth Kimmerle. During the depression, sugary snacks were marketed as cheap nutrition, more meal than treat--thus the birth of the Chicken Dinner candy bar, which came no closer to poultry than the wrapper, which sported an image of a steaming roasted chicken. Similarly, Goo Goo (pictured above) could see you through lean times. And even though The Capitol Steps generally tend toward the clotty, we were grateful for them as darkness fell. Gay Dean Believer, indeed.
iPod played: The Best of the Waitresses, California Guitar Trio - Rocks the West (Their "Ode to Joy" is worth the price of admission.)
Snoozed at: The Davenport Hotel
